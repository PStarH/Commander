# GitLab Product Marketing Meeting Transcript
**Date:** June 28, 2021
**Time:** 10:00 AM - 11:30 AM PST
**Attendees:** Sarah (PM), Mike (Engineering), Lisa (Marketing), David (Sales), Karen (Design)

---

**Sarah:** Good morning everyone. Let's kick off our Q3 product marketing review. First agenda item - our DevSecOps launch next month. Lisa, where are we on the messaging?

**Lisa:** Thanks Sarah. We've finalized the core messaging framework. The key value prop is "shift security left without slowing down." We're positioning against GitHub's Dependabot and Snyk. Our differentiator is the integrated vulnerability management within the merge request workflow.

**Mike:** From engineering side, we've completed the SAST and DAST integration. The container scanning is in beta - we're seeing 40% faster scan times compared to standalone tools.

**David:** Sales feedback - enterprise customers are asking about compliance reporting. Can we add SOC 2 and HIPAA compliance dashboards to the feature list?

**Sarah:** Good call David. Karen, can we get those dashboards designed by mid-July?

**Karen:** Yes, I have the wireframes ready. The compliance dashboard will show real-time compliance status, audit logs, and remediation tracking. I'll share the designs by end of week.

**Lisa:** For the launch campaign, we're planning:
1. A virtual event on July 15th with live demos
2. Blog series: "5 Ways to Secure Your CI/CD Pipeline"
3. Partner webinars with AWS and GCP
4. Customer case studies featuring Goldman Sachs and Siemens

**Mike:** We should also highlight the auto-remediation feature. When a vulnerability is detected, we can automatically create a merge request with the fix. That's a game-changer.

**David:** Agreed. Our competitive win rate against Snyk is already 65%. With auto-remediation, I think we can push that to 75%.

**Sarah:** What about pricing? We discussed bundling security features into the Ultimate tier.

**Lisa:** Final decision: SAST and container scanning included in Ultimate. DAST and compliance dashboards available as add-ons at $29/user/month. This gives us a clear upgrade path.

**Karen:** One concern - the UI for vulnerability management needs some polish. The current design has too many clicks to get from detection to remediation. I propose a one-click fix flow.

**Mike:** Engineering can implement that. We'll need 2 sprints. Can we delay the beta by one week?

**Sarah:** Yes, let's prioritize the UX improvement. Better to launch with a polished experience. New beta date: July 8th. Launch date stays July 15th.

**Lisa:** For the press release, I'm drafting with TechCrunch and VentureBeat exclusives. We're also submitting for Gartner's Cool Vendor recognition.

**David:** One more thing - we need to update our competitive battle cards. The current ones are from Q1 and don't include GitHub's new code scanning features.

**Sarah:** Lisa, can you update the battle cards by July 1st?

**Lisa:** Yes, I'll coordinate with David on the sales intelligence. We'll have updated cards for the July 15th launch.

**Sarah:** Great. Let's move to agenda item 2 - our SMB growth strategy. We're seeing 30% month-over-month growth in free tier signups but only 5% conversion to paid.

**David:** The main friction point is the credit card requirement for the trial. Can we offer a 30-day no-credit-card trial?

**Lisa:** That's a good idea. We could also add in-product prompts highlighting features they're missing in the free tier.

**Mike:** We could implement usage-based triggers. When a team exceeds 1000 CI minutes or 5GB storage, show them an upgrade prompt with ROI calculations.

**Karen:** I'll design an upgrade flow that shows exactly what they're missing. Side-by-side comparison of their current usage vs. what they'd get with Premium.

**Sarah:** Perfect. Let's target August 1st for the no-credit-card trial launch. David, what conversion rate improvement do you expect?

**David:** Industry benchmarks show 20-30% improvement with no-credit-card trials. I'd conservatively estimate we can get from 5% to 7% conversion.

**Sarah:** That would add $1.2M in ARR. Let's make it happen. Any other items?

**Mike:** Just one - we should consider adding a community tier for open source projects. GitHub has this and it's a major differentiator for developer mindshare.

**Sarah:** Good point. Let's scope that for Q4. For now, let's focus on the DevSecOps launch and SMB growth.

**Lisa:** One last thing - we need to update the website homepage. The current hero message is outdated. I suggest: "The complete DevSecOps platform for modern development teams."

**Karen:** I'll have the new homepage design ready by July 5th.

**Sarah:** Excellent. Let's recap action items:
1. Karen - compliance dashboard designs by July 1st, homepage by July 5th
2. Mike - one-click fix flow implementation, beta delayed to July 8th
3. Lisa - competitive battle cards by July 1st, press release drafts
4. David - sales enablement materials for DevSecOps launch
5. All - review the SMB conversion strategy and provide feedback by July 5th

Meeting adjourned. Thanks everyone!
