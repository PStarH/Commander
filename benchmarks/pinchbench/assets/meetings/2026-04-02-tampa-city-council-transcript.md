[2026-04-02 09:02:30] PUBLIC COMMENT - Item 1: Downtown Parking Resolution
Speaker: Robert Chen, Downtown Business Alliance
"We support the proposed parking resolution but request extended hours for meter enforcement to 8 PM instead of 6 PM. This would better serve our restaurant and retail businesses."

Speaker: Maria Santos, Tampa Residents Association
"We oppose the extended hours. Many residents rely on free evening parking for downtown events. We suggest a compromise of 7 PM."

[2026-04-02 09:10:00] COUNCIL DISCUSSION - Downtown Parking
Council Member Johnson: "I've received over 200 emails on this issue. The business community is split."
Council Member Brown: "Can we add an amendment for 7 PM with a 6-month review period?"
Council Member Garcia: "I support the amendment. We need data to make an informed long-term decision."
Chairperson Martinez: "Let's vote on the amended resolution."

[2026-04-02 09:15:00] VOTE - Downtown Parking Resolution (Amended)
Resolution 2026-042-A: Extended meter enforcement to 7 PM with 6-month review.
Yes: Martinez, Brown, Garcia, Davis, Wilson (5)
No: Johnson, Williams (2)
Motion carries 5-2.

[2026-04-02 09:20:00] ITEM 2: Budget Amendment - Infrastructure Fund
City Manager Thompson presents: "We are requesting a $2.3 million amendment to the Infrastructure Fund for emergency road repairs on Bayshore Boulevard following the February flooding."

Council Member Williams: "What's the timeline for completion?"
City Manager Thompson: "Phase 1 by August 2026, Phase 2 by December 2026."

Council Member Davis: "Where is the funding coming from?"
Finance Director Patel: "From the reserve fund, which currently has $18.7 million."

[2026-04-02 09:35:00] VOTE - Budget Amendment
Motion by Williams, seconded by Garcia, to approve $2.3M infrastructure budget amendment.
Yes: All (7-0)
Motion carries unanimously.

[2026-04-02 09:40:00] ITEM 3: Zoning Variance - 1400 N Florida Avenue
Applicant: Tampa Development Partners LLC
Request: Variance to allow 12-story mixed-use development in zoned 8-story maximum area.

Planning Director Kim: "Staff recommends denial. The variance exceeds the comprehensive plan guidelines by 50%."

Speaker: James Wright, representing Tampa Development Partners
"We've reduced the height from 15 stories to 12. This project will bring 200 affordable housing units and create 500 jobs."

Speaker: Linda Park, Neighborhood Association President
"This would set a dangerous precedent. Our neighborhood plan specifically limits height to preserve character."

[2026-04-02 10:00:00] COUNCIL DISCUSSION - Zoning Variance
Council Member Wilson: "The affordable housing component is compelling. Can we approve with conditions?"
Council Member Brown: "What conditions would address the height concern?"
Planning Director Kim: "We could approve a 10-story maximum with mandatory affordable housing at 30% of units."

[2026-04-02 10:15:00] VOTE - Zoning Variance (Modified)
Motion by Wilson, seconded by Brown, to approve variance with 10-story maximum and 30% affordable housing requirement.
Yes: Martinez, Wilson, Brown, Davis (4)
No: Johnson, Williams, Garcia (3)
Motion carries 4-3.

[2026-04-02 10:20:00] ITEM 4: Public Transit Expansion Proposal
Transit Director Adams presents Phase 2 bus rapid transit plan: "This would add 3 new BRT corridors connecting downtown to Westshore, Brandon, and Temple Terrace. Estimated cost: $45 million over 3 years."

Council Member Garcia: "What's the ridership projection?"
Transit Director Adams: "We project 15,000 daily riders by Year 2, increasing to 25,000 by Year 5."

Council Member Johnson: "I'd like to see a public-private partnership model explored before committing public funds."

[2026-04-02 10:45:00] ACTION ITEM - Transit Proposal
Motion by Garcia, seconded by Davis, to direct staff to develop a detailed P3 proposal for BRT expansion and return within 60 days.
Yes: All (7-0)
Motion carries unanimously.
